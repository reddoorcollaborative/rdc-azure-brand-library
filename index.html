02 — Design Principles

Build in Public

Azure's visual identity is guided by six design principles that shape every creative decision — from photography and typography to 3D graphic language and layout composition.

01
🔭
Immersion

The world of Azure is rich in activity and detail, taking place in both physical and conceptual spaces. Immersion allows us to dive in and glimpse those scenes in captivating detail.

02
∞
Infinite

The limitless innovation of Azure is experienced through vast scale — both in terms of possibilities (3D unlimited data flows) and capabilities (cinematic wide views of physical spaces).

03
⚙️
Expert

The deep technical expertise required to build and maintain Azure is expressed through photography of scenes and team members, schematic illustrations, and purposeful headlines.

04
🔬
Curious

A relentless spirit of innovation and desire to solve complex problems. Close-up and macro photography views feature the beautiful details seen in frontier technology.

05
→
Forward

Azure's forward momentum is expressed in photographic scenes through central focal points and moving subjects, as well as movement of objects in 3D spaces.

06
👤
Authentic

The brand features real people and places. It's expressed using traits of the Microsoft brand to create a rich visual world unique to Azure — never overly staged or synthetic.

03 — Logo

Logo Usage

The positive (full-color) logo is the preferred primary logo. Reverse and single-color versions are available for specific backgrounds. A secondary stacked logo is available for tight spaces.

Positive Logo · Light Background (Primary)
Reverse Logo · Dark Background (Primary)
Single-color · Color Background (Medium value)
Color Logo · Dark Color Background (Dark value)

Clearspace

Maintain a minimum clearspace of 1× the logo symbol size on all sides.

Layout Margins

Preserve a minimum margin of 1× or 2× the logo symbol in design layouts.

Product Icon ≠ Logo

The Azure product icon is not a logo. Never use it in marketing materials — use the full Azure logo.

06 — Photography

Photography Guidelines

Azure photography spans seven categories. Each emphasizes one or more design principles — prioritizing authenticity, immersion, and technical expertise over staged stock imagery.

Developers

Authentic, immersive shots of developers in focused work. Avoid staged studio shots. Subjects should appear natural, relaxed, and confident — cropped intimately using the Immersion principle.

Immersive crops Focused expression Hint of code on screen Authentic styling
IT Decision Makers

Professional expertise and optimism. ITDM audience seen in clean, open, naturally-lit environments. Business casual attire. Moments of collaboration or deep work.

Natural expression Workplace setting Business casual Collaborative body language
Industries: People Focus

Azure serves healthcare, finance, retail, manufacturing, education, and more. Shots are graphic, striking, with attention to detail — featuring people working alone or collaborating.

Graphic & striking Detail & contrast Innovative environment
Industries: Graphic Details

Tech-focused close-up shots of partner technology. Dynamic lighting, crisp and clean. Flexible on camera depth of field and point of view to highlight innovation.

Tech-focused Dynamic lighting Crisp & modern
Data Centers: Exterior

Billion-dollar facilities shown with the scale of vast spaces. Should appear modern, premium, and state-of-the-art — reinforcing the Infinite design principle.

Vast scale Premium & modern Infinite principle
Data Centers: Interior & Details

Interior shots emphasize computing power and infinite development possibilities. Detail shots use macro and close-up views to highlight hardware precision and the Curious principle.

Computing power Macro & close-up Curious principle

Key Photography Don'ts

✗ Avoid

Overly posed models in a studio setting with dramatic or colored lighting

✗ Avoid

Obvious stock photography aesthetics — glossy wardrobes and brand-new gear

✗ Avoid

Showing specific code or proprietary technology that limits application breadth

07 — Graphic Language

Holding Shapes

Rounded corner UI containers used to crop photography and 3D scenes. They add immersion by zooming into compelling focal areas rather than showing a full image. Shapes must always align to 90° angles.

Construction Rule

Round corners until one edge becomes a perfect semi-circle — no flat segments on the short sides. Then use the shape to crop the image.

Sizing Ratios

Follow estimated length-to-width ratios. Avoid shapes that are too narrow, form a perfect circle, or use non-standard angles.

Montage Format

Multiple images can be combined in a larger holding shape. Ensure visible division between images and sufficient contrast with the background color.

Holding Shape — What to Avoid

✗ Too narrow

Holding shapes that are excessively long and narrow lose the rounded end effect.

✗ Perfect circle

A holding shape should never become a perfect circle — ratio must be elongated.

✗ Custom shapes

Only use approved holding shape constructions — no custom organic or irregular shapes.

✗ Non-90° angles

Holding shapes must always be aligned to 90° angles. Diagonal orientations are off-brand.

Marketing Funnel Guidance

Attract

Top-level brand communications — social post copy, carousel first frame, ebook cover. Pique curiosity with Primary layouts.

Inform

Web pages, carousel inner frames, ebook interiors. More copy allowed. Use Secondary layouts.

Convert

Product detail pages and CTAs. Combine brand visuals with specific product benefit messaging.

Engage

Community, developer content, events. Emphasize Authentic and Curious principles.
